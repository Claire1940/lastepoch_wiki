## Online vs. Offline: What the Charts Don't Show

One nuance that many players overlook when analyzing the **last epoch steam charts** is the game's robust offline functionality. Unlike many modern ARPGs that require a constant internet connection, Last Epoch offers two distinct ways to play without being tethered to a server.

1.  **Play Offline:** Selected from the main menu. Requires an initial login but allows for chat access.
2.  **True Offline:** Launched directly through Steam properties. No internet connection is required at any point.

Because "True Offline" mode does not require a connection to the game's matchmaking servers, these players are sometimes underrepresented or handled differently by third-party scrapers, though Steam still tracks the application as "running." Furthermore, many players opted for offline characters during the 1.0 launch to bypass server queues. This means the actual number of people enjoying Last Epoch is often higher than the concurrent "online" player count suggests.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Characters created in Offline mode cannot be transferred to Online mode later. If you care about the economy or playing with friends, ensure you start an Online character even if player counts are currently high.
