## Choosing Your Path: Classes and Masteries

One of the standout features of the game is its rigid yet flexible class system. You begin your journey as one of five base classes, but the real depth opens up when you ascend into a Mastery Class. In total, there are 15 Masteries available, each providing a distinct flavor of gameplay.

Unlike some other titles where classes can feel like mere skins for the same skills, this game locks specific power fantasies behind its Masteries. However, the skill trees within those Masteries allow for incredible diversity. For example, a Sentinel can become a Void Knight, specializing in dark echoes and spatial manipulation, or a Paladin focused on holy fire and healing.

| Base Class | Masteries | Primary Playstyle |
|---|---|---|
| **Sentinel** | Void Knight, Forge Guard, Paladin | Melee, Tanking, Holy/Void Magic |
| **Mage** | Spellblade, Sorcerer, Runemaster | Elemental Casting, Enchanted Melee |
| **Acolyte** | Lich, Necromancer, Warlock | Minions, Curses, Life Sacrifice |
| **Primalist** | Beastmaster, Shaman, Druid | Shapeshifting, Totems, Companions |
| **Rogue** | Marksman, Bladedancer, Falconer | High Mobility, Bows, Traps |

> **💡 Tip:** Your Mastery choice is permanent. While you can respec your skill points and passives easily, you cannot change your Mastery once it is chosen. Choose wisely based on the end-game fantasy you prefer!

## Deterministic Crafting: Reforging the Future

The crafting system in this game is often cited as its "secret sauce." Unlike the high-variance, "gambling" style of crafting found in other ARPGs, this system is highly deterministic. You aren't just pulling a lever and hoping for the best; you are actively building an item.

By using Glyphs and Runes, you can manipulate specific affixes on your gear. If you find a pair of boots with a high movement speed roll but a useless resistance, you can use a Rune of Shattering to break it down for parts or a Glyph of Chaos to reroll the bad stat into something potentially better.

| Crafting Item | Function | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| **Rune of Shattering** | Destroys an item to grant shards of its affixes. | Farming specific rare shards (e.g., +Level to Skills). |
| **Glyph of Hope** | Grants a 25% chance to consume no Forging Potential. | Essential for every high-tier craft to extend item life. |
| **Glyph of Chaos** | Randomly changes an affix when upgrading it. | Turning a "dead" stat into a useful one during leveling. |
| **Rune of Ascendance** | Changes an item into a random Unique of the same type. | Targeting specific build-enabling Unique items. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Every item has a limited amount of **Forging Potential**. Once this hits zero, the item can no longer be modified. Always prioritize your most important stats first!

## FAQ

**Q: Is Last Epoch on Steam "Pay-to-Win"?**

A: No. Eleventh Hour Games has maintained a strict "Zero Pay-to-Win" policy. All real-money purchases are purely cosmetic and do not provide gameplay advantages, experience boosts, or gear.

**Q: Can I play Last Epoch offline?**

A: Yes, the **last epoch steam** version includes a fully functional True Offline mode. You can create characters that do not require an internet connection, though these characters cannot participate in online leaderboards or trading.

**Q: How does the multiplayer work?**

A: The game supports online co-op for up to four players. You can join friends seamlessly, and loot is instanced, meaning you don't have to fight over drops.

**Q: Are there seasons or leagues?**

A: Yes, the game uses a "Cycle" system. Each Cycle introduces new mechanics, balance changes, and a fresh economy. Your characters from previous Cycles are moved to the "Legacy" realm, similar to how seasons work in other major ARPGs.
