## Understanding Exalted Rarity and Tiers

In Last Epoch, item rarity follows a strict progression. Exalted items are distinguished by their purple color and the presence of at least one affix at Tier 6 or Tier 7. Unlike Tiers 1 through 5, which can be manually upgraded at the Forge using Affix Shards, T6 and T7 affixes are "drop-only." This makes the initial drop significantly more impactful than lower-rarity items.

| Rarity | Color | Max Affix Tier | Craftable? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Magic** | Blue | Tier 5 | Yes |
| **Rare** | Yellow | Tier 5 | Yes |
| **Exalted** | Purple | **Tier 7** | Yes (T1-T5 only) |
| **Unique** | Orange | Fixed | No |
| **Legendary** | Red | Tier 7 | Via Eternity Cache |

> **💡 Tip:** You cannot use a Rune of Refinement to turn a T5 affix into a T6. You must find the item with the T6 or T7 mod already present on the base.

Exalted items serve two primary purposes: they are often the "Best in Slot" (BiS) items for non-unique gear slots, and they act as the essential "fodder" for creating Legendary items. When you combine an Exalted item with a Unique item that has Legendary Potential (LP) in the Eternity Cache, the Unique "consumes" random affixes from the Exalted item, inheriting their power.

## How to Farm Last Epoch Exalted Items

Farming for specific exalted mods has become significantly more streamlined with the updates in 2026. While random drops occur throughout the Monolith of Fate, high-efficiency players utilize specific mechanics to "print" high-potential bases.

### The Nemesis Empowerment Strategy
The Nemesis system is one of the most reliable ways to generate double and triple exalted items. By interacting with Nemesis encounters and choosing to "Empower" the items within, you increase their Forging Potential and the tier of their affixes.

1.  **Find a Nemesis:** Locate a Nemesis encounter in a Monolith echo.
2.  **Check the Loot:** If the item matches your desired base (e.g., a specific helmet or weapon), choose to Empower it.
3.  **Repeat:** Find the Nemesis again in subsequent echoes. Each empowerment adds more power, eventually turning standard items into multi-exalted powerhouses.
4.  **Claim:** Once the item reaches T6 or T7 in the desired slots, defeat the Nemesis to claim your prize.

### Weaver’s Tree and Echoing Riches
For those pushing deep into the endgame, the Weaver Tree offers a "seed" strategy. By using nodes like **Echoing Riches** and **Sealed Riches**, you can influence the game to drop items similar to a "seed" item you have imprinted. If you imprint a high-forging potential double exalted helmet, the system will increase the likelihood of similar helmets dropping from Lost Caches within the Monolith.

## Advanced Crafting Techniques

Once you have acquired your **last epoch exalted items**, the real work begins at the Forge (F). Because T6 and T7 mods cannot be altered, your goal is to optimize the remaining three affix slots without depleting the item's Forging Potential.

### Essential Crafting Components
Using the right Glyphs and Runes determines whether an item becomes a masterpiece or vendor trash.

| Component | Effect on Exalted Items | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Glyph of Hope** | 25% chance to cost 0 Forging Potential. | **Mandatory** for every single craft. |
| **Glyph of Chaos** | Randomizes an affix when upgrading. | Use on "dead" T1/T2 mods to find useful stats. |
| **Glyph of Despair** | Chance to seal an affix into a 5th slot. | Creating "5-affix" items by moving a utility mod. |
| **Glyph of Insight** | Changes a prefix to an Experimental mod. | Exclusive to Boots, Gloves, and Belts. |
| **Rune of Removal** | Removes a random affix and gives shards. | High-risk "cleansing" of a bad mod on a T7 base. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Always prioritize upgrading your most important affixes first. If you run out of Forging Potential while working on a resistance mod, you might leave your primary damage stat stuck at a low tier.

### Sealing Affixes for Maximum Power
The **Glyph of Despair** is the secret weapon of pro crafters. When you use it to upgrade a low-tier affix (ideally Tier 1 or 2), there is a chance the mod will be moved to a special "sealed" slot. This locks the mod but opens up the original slot for a new affix. This is the only way to have five functional affixes on a single exalted item.

## Experimental Affixes and Exiled Mages
Exalted items can also spawn with—or be crafted to include—Experimental affixes. These are unique modifiers found only on items dropped by Exiled Mages or created using a **Glyph of Insight**.

*   **Boots:** Can gain "Haste on Traversal Skill Use."
*   **Gloves:** Can gain "Ward per Missing Health."
*   **Belts:** Can gain "Ward on Potion Use."

To maximize these, use a **Rune of Research** to seal an Experimental affix with 100% certainty, provided the item has enough Forging Potential. This allows you to build a standard exalted item around a powerful, specialized mechanic.

## Preparing Items for the Eternity Cache
The ultimate fate for many high-tier **last epoch exalted items** is to be sacrificed for a Legendary. When preparing an item for this process, focus on the following:

1.  **Affix Count:** The Exalted item *must* have four unsealed affixes to be used in the Eternity Cache.
2.  **Tier Quality:** Since the Unique item will pull affixes randomly (based on its LP), you want all four affixes on your Exalted item to be as high as possible (T5+). 
3.  **Clean Bases:** If your Exalted item has a "dead" stat (e.g., Mana Regen on a manaless build), try to use a Rune of Removal or Glyph of Chaos to change it before smashing it into a Unique.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I turn a Rare item into an Exalted item?**

A: No. Exalted status is defined by having a Tier 6 or Tier 7 affix, and these tiers cannot be reached through the standard crafting process. They must drop from enemies, chests, or be generated via the Nemesis system.

**Q: Why did my Glyph of Despair fail to seal the affix?**

A: The chance to seal an affix is higher at lower tiers (T1 or T2) and higher on items with more affixes. If you try to seal a T4 or T5 mod, the success rate is extremely low.

**Q: What is the difference between a T6 and a T7 affix?**

A: T6 affixes are powerful, but T7 affixes represent the absolute maximum roll for a stat. T7 mods are significantly rarer and usually only appear in high-corruption Monoliths or from empowered Nemesis encounters.

**Q: Should I pick up every purple item I see?**

A: In the early endgame, yes. However, as you progress, you should use the Loot Filter (Shift+F) to highlight **last epoch exalted items** that contain specific T6/T7 affixes relevant to your build or high-value shards (like +Level to Skills).
