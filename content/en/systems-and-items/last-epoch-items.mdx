## Item Rarities and Progression

Every item that drops in Eterra falls into a specific rarity tier. These tiers determine not only the number of affixes an item can have but also its "Forging Potential," which acts as the resource for any modifications you wish to make in the Forge.

| Rarity | Color | Max Affixes | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Common** | White | 0 | Basic items with no modifiers. Used mostly as crafting bases. |
| **Magic** | Blue | 1-2 | Basic modifiers. Common in early leveling. |
| **Rare** | Yellow | 3-4 | The standard gear for mid-game. Can have up to Tier 5 affixes. |
| **Exalted** | Purple | 4+ | Contains at least one **Tier 6 or Tier 7** affix that cannot be crafted. |
| **Unique** | Orange | Fixed | Special items with build-defining effects. Cannot be modified normally. |
| **Set** | Green | Fixed | Part of a collection that grants bonuses when multiple pieces are worn. |
| **Legendary** | Red | Variable | The result of combining a Unique with an Exalted item. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep an eye out for Exalted items (Purple). Even if the base item isn't for your class, the Tier 6 or 7 affixes are required for endgame Legendary crafting.

## The Core of Crafting: Forging Potential

In Last Epoch, crafting is done through the Forge (accessible by pressing **F** at any time). Every non-unique item has a stat called **Forging Potential**. Think of this as the "durability" of the item's soul. Every time you add an affix, upgrade a tier, or use a rune, a random amount of Forging Potential is consumed. Once it hits zero, the item is "bricked" and cannot be modified further.

### Understanding Affixes
Items can generally have four main affixes:
1.  **Two Prefixes:** Usually offensive or utility stats (e.g., Strength, Fire Damage, Cast Speed).
2.  **Two Suffixes:** Usually defensive or resistance stats (e.g., Health, Void Resistance, Dodge).

Using a **Glyph of Despair**, you can occasionally "seal" a fifth affix, moving it to a special slot and freeing up one of the four main slots for a new modifier.

## Essential Crafting Materials: Runes and Glyphs

To modify your **last epoch items**, you need specific consumables. These are divided into Runes (which perform actions) and Glyphs (which modify the outcome of an action).

### Common Runes and Their Uses

| Rune | Effect | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Shattering** | Destroys the item to grant a random number of its affix shards. | Use on items with rare affixes you need for your stash. |
| **Removal** | Removes one random affix and grants all shards for that affix. | Better than shattering if you need a specific, high-tier shard. |
| **Refinement** | Rerolls the values of all affixes within their current tiers. | Use this last to "perfect" a high-tier item. |
| **Ascendance** | Turns a non-unique item into a random Unique of the same type. | A great way to target-farm build-enabling uniques like rings or belts. |
| **Discovery** | Fills empty affix slots with random Tier 1 affixes for 0 potential cost. | Excellent for early-game leveling gear. |

### Powerful Glyphs

*   **Glyph of Hope:** Grants a 25% chance that a craft consumes zero Forging Potential. You should use this on almost every single craft.
*   **Glyph of Chaos:** Upgrades an affix tier but changes the affix to a different random one. Use this when you have a great item with one "dead" stat.
*   **Glyph of Order:** Prevents the roll of an affix from changing within its range when upgraded.
*   **Glyph of Despair:** Has a chance to seal an affix into its own slot, allowing for a 5-affix item. The chance is higher on lower-tier affixes (Tier 1-2).

## Advanced Gear: Uniques and Legendaries

While Rare and Exalted items are the backbone of your build, Uniques provide the "flavor" and power spikes. However, the true endgame involves **Legendary items**.

### Legendary Potential (LP)
Some Unique items drop with "Legendary Potential" (ranging from 1 to 4). This indicates how many affixes it can "absorb" from an Exalted item. To create a Legendary, you must:
1.  Find a Unique with at least 1 Legendary Potential.
2.  Find an Exalted item of the **same item type** (e.g., both must be One-Handed Swords) with four affixes.
3.  Complete the **Temporal Sanctum** dungeon.
4.  Place both items in the Eternity Cache at the end of the dungeon.

The resulting Legendary will have all the Unique's original stats plus a random selection of affixes from the Exalted item, equal to the amount of LP the Unique had.

### Weaver's Will
A rarer type of Unique item is the **Weaver's Will** variant. These items do not have LP. Instead, as you gain experience while wearing them, the item "grows," randomly adding and upgrading affixes until its Weaver's Will count reaches zero. These can result in incredibly powerful items that are entirely unique to your character.

## Idols and Specialized Equipment

Beyond standard gear slots, **last epoch items** include Idols. These are placed in a dedicated 4x4 grid that expands as you progress through the campaign.

*   **Class Specific:** Many Idols only provide bonuses to specific classes (e.g., "Chance to summon a Totem on hit" for Primatists).
*   **Build Defining:** Idols often provide stats that aren't available on standard gear, such as "Increased area of effect for Smite" or "Health Leeched as Mana."
*   **Tetris Management:** Because Idols come in different shapes (1x1, 1x2, 2x2, 1x4, etc.), managing your Idol grid is a mini-game in itself to maximize efficiency.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not ignore your Idol inventory. A well-optimized grid can often provide as much power as two or three main gear pieces combined.

## Target Farming and Loot Filters

With thousands of **last epoch items** dropping, using a Loot Filter (Shift+F) is mandatory. A good filter should:
1.  **Hide** items that are irrelevant to your class.
2.  **Recolor** items with high-tier affixes (T6/T7).
3.  **Highlight** specific bases you need for crafting (e.g., "Crystal Swords" for spell casters).

## FAQ

**Q: What is the most important stat on last epoch items?**

A: While it depends on your build, **Health** and **Resistances** are universally important for survival. Offensively, look for **Critical Strike Chance** and **Attributes** (Strength, Intelligence, etc.) that scale your main skills.

**Q: Can I remove a "bricked" item's affixes?**

A: No. Once an item has zero Forging Potential, it cannot be modified. Your only options are to wear it as-is, sell it, or use a Rune of Shattering to try and recover some of the shards.

**Q: How do I get more Tier 7 affixes?**

A: Tier 7 affixes only drop in high-level areas, typically in Empowered Monoliths (Level 100). They cannot be crafted manually; they must drop naturally on Exalted items.

**Q: Does item level matter more than affixes?**

A: Not necessarily. In Last Epoch, the "Base" of the item (the implicit stats at the top) is often more important than the item level. A lower-level base with perfect T5 affixes is usually better than a high-level base with useless stats.
