## What is Legendary Potential?

Legendary Potential (LP) is a rare property found on Unique items that dictates their ability to be upgraded into Legendary items. Every Unique item in Last Epoch has a hidden "Legendary Potential Level," which influences how likely it is to drop with LP. When an item drops with this potential, it will have a value ranging from 1 to 4.

The number of LP points on a Unique item directly corresponds to how many affixes it will "absorb" from an Exalted item during the crafting process. For example, a Unique with 2 LP will randomly take two affixes from a four-affix Exalted item and add them to its own stat pool.

### Legendary Potential Levels Explained

| LP Level | Affixes Transferred | Rarity | Potential Power |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0 LP** | None | Common | Baseline Unique performance. |
| **1 LP** | 1 Random Affix | Uncommon | Significant boost; easy to find. |
| **2 LP** | 2 Random Affixes | Rare | Endgame standard for most builds. |
| **3 LP** | 3 Random Affixes | Very Rare | Massive power spike; requires heavy farming. |
| **4 LP** | 4 All Affixes | Extremely Rare | Perfect item; transfers the entire Exalted item. |

> **💡 Tip:** Even a 1 LP item can be game-changing if you manage to transfer a Tier 7 (T7) Exalted affix like "Level of [Skill]" or "Hybrid Health."

## The Temporal Sanctum: Where Legendaries are Born

To utilize your **last epoch legendary potential**, you must travel to the Temporal Sanctum. This dungeon is the only place in Eterra where the Eternity Cache exists—a device capable of merging items across timelines.

### Preparing for the Dungeon
Before entering, you must possess a **Temporal Sanctum Key**, which typically drops from Timeline Bosses in the Monolith of Fate or as random world drops. The dungeon features a unique mechanic: the **Temporal Shift (Default key: D)**. This allows you to swap between the Ruined and Divine eras to bypass obstacles and navigate the floors.

### Eternity Cache Requirements
Once you defeat Julra, the dungeon boss, you will gain access to the Eternity Cache. To create a Legendary, you must place two items inside:
1.  **A Unique Item:** Must have at least 1 Legendary Potential.
2.  **An Exalted Item:** Must be the **exact same item type** (e.g., a Two-Handed Sword for a Two-Handed Sword unique) and must have **four unsealed affixes**.

## The "Slamming" Process: Step-by-Step

The community refers to the merging process as "slamming." Since 2024 updates, the system has become more deterministic if you run higher tiers of the dungeon.

### Dungeon Tier Restrictions
The level of the Unique item you wish to upgrade determines which dungeon tier you must complete.

| Dungeon Tier | Unique Level Requirement | Special Mechanics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tier 1** | Level 50 or lower | Standard random transfer. |
| **Tier 2** | Level 65 or lower | **Guaranteed Affix Selection** unlocked. |
| **Tier 3** | Level 75 or lower | Guaranteed Affix Selection. |
| **Tier 4** | No Level Limit | Guaranteed Affix Selection; highest difficulty. |

### How to Guarantee an Affix
When using the Eternity Cache in Tier 2 or higher, you are no longer at the mercy of total randomness for your first affix. You can select one specific affix from your Exalted item to be guaranteed to transfer to the Unique. The remaining LP slots (if any) will then be filled randomly from the remaining pool.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Sealed affixes (affixes moved to a special fifth slot via a Glyph of Despair) **cannot** be transferred to a Legendary item. Only the four standard prefixes and suffixes are eligible.

## Farming High Legendary Potential Uniques

Finding items with 3 or 4 LP is the ultimate endgame chase. The likelihood of an item dropping with high **last epoch legendary potential** depends on several scaling factors.

1.  **The Item's Effective Level for LP:** Every Unique has an internal "LP rating." High-level Uniques like *Omnis* or *Raven's Void* have very low chances of rolling LP, whereas low-level Uniques like *Firestarter's Torch* roll LP 3 or 4 quite frequently.
2.  **Corruption Levels:** Higher Corruption in the Monolith of Fate increases the rarity of drops, indirectly boosting the chance of seeing LP on Uniques.
3.  **Area Level:** You are significantly more likely to find LP uniques in Level 90+ zones and Empowered Monoliths.
4.  **Circle of Fortune (CoF):** If you join the Circle of Fortune faction, Rank 8 provides a massive bonus that **doubles the chance** of Uniques dropping with Legendary Potential.

### Using the Nemesis System
Introduced in recent cycles, the Nemesis system offers a secondary way to interact with LP. If you find a Nemesis encounter containing an **Egg of the Forgotten**, you can place a 0 LP Unique inside. By "Empowering" the Nemesis in subsequent encounters, you have a chance to add Legendary Potential to that item or even transform it directly into a Legendary with random affixes.

## Advanced Crafting: The Perfect Exalted Base

A Legendary is only as good as the Exalted item used to create it. To optimize your **last epoch legendary potential** crafts, you should treat the Exalted item as a project.

*   **Rune of Creation:** If you find a "god-roll" Exalted item (e.g., T7 Critical Strike Multiplier and T5 Attack Speed), use a Rune of Creation to duplicate it. This gives you two attempts at the "slam" if your first Unique fails to hit the desired random affixes. Note that this requires the item to have at least 1 Forging Potential remaining.
*   **Glyph of Hope:** Always use Glyphs of Hope when preparing your Exalted base to preserve Forging Potential for as many Tier 5 upgrades as possible.
*   **Rune of Havoc:** If your Exalted item has one amazing T7 stat but three useless ones, use Runes of Havoc to shuffle the tiers and types, hoping to turn those dead stats into something useful like Health or Resistances before the slam.

## Legendary Potential vs. Weaver's Will

It is important to distinguish between LP and Weaver's Will. An item **cannot** have both.
*   **Legendary Potential:** Requires an Exalted item and a dungeon run. You have high control over the outcome through crafting the Exalted base.
*   **Weaver's Will:** These items "grow" as you kill enemies while wearing them. They gain random affixes and upgrade them up to Tier 7 automatically. You have zero control over which stats appear, making them a pure gamble compared to the LP system.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use a Unique with 0 Legendary Potential in the Eternity Cache?**

A: No. The Eternity Cache requires at least 1 LP to function. If you have a 0 LP Unique you want to upgrade, your only option is to use the Nemesis "Egg of the Forgotten" system to try and add LP to it.

**Q: Does the Forging Potential of the Exalted item matter for the slam?**

A: No. You can use an Exalted item with 0 Forging Potential. As long as it has four unsealed affixes and is the correct item type, it will work perfectly in the Eternity Cache.

**Q: What happens to the Exalted item after the craft?**

A: The Exalted item is destroyed in the process. The Unique item is transformed into a Legendary item, retaining its original Unique stats and gaining the specified number of affixes from the destroyed Exalted item.

**Q: Can I slam a class-specific affix onto a Unique that any class can wear?**

A: Yes, but be careful. If you slam a Mage-specific affix (like "Level of Meteor") onto a generic Unique ring, that ring will become class-locked to the Mage. Ensure you only do this if the item is intended for that specific character.

**Q: Is there any way to reroll the affixes on a Legendary item?**

A: No. Once a "slam" is complete, the affixes are permanent. If you are unhappy with the result, you must find a new Unique with **last epoch legendary potential** and a new Exalted item to try again. This is why using a Rune of Creation on your Exalted base is highly recommended for 1LP and 2LP items.
