## Core Features of Modern Build Planners

A high-quality **last epoch build planner** does more than just show a skill tree. It acts as a full-scale character simulator. In the current 2026 meta, several features are considered essential for any serious theorycrafter.

### Skill and Passive Tree Simulation
Every class in Last Epoch has access to a base class tree and three mastery trees. The planner allows you to allocate points and see exactly how nodes interact. For example, if a node grants "Increased Damage per point of Intelligence," the planner will calculate your actual damage output based on the gear you have equipped in the simulator.

### The Weaver Tree
Introduced in recent updates, the Weaver Tree adds another layer of customization. Planners now include a dedicated tab for Weaver Tree progression, allowing you to simulate the RNG-heavy upgrades of Weaver’s Will items. This is crucial for determining if a specific unique item is worth the investment over a high-tier Exalted item.

## How to Import Your Character

One of the most powerful aspects of a **last epoch build planner** is the ability to import your live character to see where improvements can be made. Depending on whether you play online or offline, the process varies slightly.

### Online Character Import
1. Open the planner and select the **Import** button.
2. Enter your Account Name (found in the social menu in-game).
3. Select the specific character from the list.
4. The planner will automatically populate your skills, passives, and currently equipped gear.

### Offline Character Import
For offline players, you must locate your character save files. In 2026, these are typically found in the following directories:

*   **Windows:** `C:/Users/[User]/AppData/LocalLow/Eleventh Hour Games/Last Epoch/Saves`
*   **Steam Deck/Linux:** `[Steam_Folder]/steamapps/userdata/[ID]/899770/ac/`

The files are usually named `1CHARACTERSLOT_BETA_#`. Uploading this file to the **last epoch build planner** will mirror your character exactly as it exists on your hard drive.

## Optimizing Stats for Shattered Omens (Season 4)

Season 4 has introduced new ailments and environmental hazards that make defensive layering more important than ever. When using a **last epoch build planner**, you should aim for specific "Goldilocks" zones for your defensive stats before focusing entirely on offense.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not ignore Physical Resistance. While Void and Necrotic damage are flashy, the majority of "one-shot" mechanics in the Shattered Omens season still rely on heavy Physical scaling.

### Defensive Stat Priority
1.  **Resistances:** Aim for the 75% cap for all types. Remember that in Last Epoch, resistances can go negative if you are under the cap in high-level areas.
2.  **Critical Strike Avoidance:** 100% is mandatory for high-tier Monoliths. Alternatively, stack "Reduced Bonus Damage from Crits."
3.  **Endurance:** Aim for a 60% Endurance cap with a threshold that covers at least 20% of your maximum health.
4.  **Armor:** Even for non-strength builds, armor provides a significant damage reduction against all hits (not just physical).

## Exporting Loot Filters and Sharing Builds

Once you have perfected your character in the **last epoch build planner**, you don't have to manually recreate your loot filter. Most planners offer an "Export Loot Filter" function. This tool analyzes the items you've equipped in the planner and generates a `.xml` file or a copy-paste string. 

When you import this string into Last Epoch, the game will automatically highlight items that have the specific affixes your build requires. This is the most efficient way to gear up in 2026, as it filters out the clutter and lets you focus on upgrades.

To share your creation with the community, simply click "Save and Get Link." This generates a unique URL that preserves every node and affix you selected, making it easy to post on forums or Discord for feedback.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use the last epoch build planner on my phone?**

A: Yes, both Last Epoch Tools and Maxroll have mobile-responsive designs. While the Maxroll planner is generally smoother on smaller screens, Last Epoch Tools provides more granular data that some veteran players prefer.

**Q: Why does the planner show higher damage than I see in-game?**

A: Planners often calculate "Average DPS" or "Effective DPS," taking into account critical hits, ailments, and conditional buffs (like "Damage while at full health") that might not be active on your in-game character sheet. Ensure the "Conditions" tab in the planner matches your current gameplay state.

**Q: Does the planner support the new Season 4 uniques?**

A: Yes, the **last epoch build planner** databases are typically updated within hours of a patch release. In 2026, the Shattered Omens uniques and the new "Omen" crafting materials are fully integrated into the simulation tools.

**Q: How do I see my "Effective Health Pool" (EHP) in the planner?**

A: Look for the "Defense" or "Calculations" tab. The planner will combine your Health, Armor, Resistances, and Ward to give you an EHP value against different damage types (Physical, Fire, etc.). This is the most accurate way to measure your survivability.
