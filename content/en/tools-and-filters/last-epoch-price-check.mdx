## How to Perform an In-Game Price Check

The Merchant's Guild has implemented a streamlined system to help players evaluate their items without needing third-party overlays. However, this system is context-sensitive and requires you to be in the right place at the right time.

To initiate a **last epoch price check**, you must travel to the Bazaar. Unlike general vendors, price checking is tied to specific item types. If you want to check the value of a pair of gloves, you must interact with the Glove Vendor specifically.

### Step-by-Step Price Checking
1.  **Travel to the Bazaar:** Use the 'Y' menu to quickly visit the Merchant’s Guild hub.
2.  **Locate the Specific Vendor:** Find the NPC that handles the item category you wish to sell (e.g., Swords, Idols, Body Armor).
3.  **Open the Search Interface:** Interact with the vendor and open your inventory (default key 'I').
4.  **Shift + Right-Click:** Hover over the item in your inventory and press **Shift + Right-Click**. This action automatically populates the search filters with the item's current affixes and base type.
5.  **Refine and Search:** Click the search button to see what similar items are currently listed for.

> **💡 Tip:** When price checking, the system often includes "Forging Potential" in the search. If you see zero results, try removing the Forging Potential requirement, as buyers often care more about the affixes than the remaining crafting flexibility on high-tier items.

## Valuing Rare and Exalted Items

Exalted items (Purple) are the backbone of the Last Epoch economy. Their value is almost entirely dependent on the Tier 6 or Tier 7 affix they carry. A "dead" Tier 6 affix like Stun Avoidance might be worth nothing, while a Tier 7 Critical Strike Multiplier could be worth millions.

To keep your stash organized, many top players use a "Tab Tier" system. Instead of doing a manual **last epoch price check** for every single drop, they dump items into tabs based on a quick visual assessment.

| Tab Category | Criteria | Estimated Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Budget Tab** | Tier 6 "okay" stats (Resistances, Health) | **20,000 - 50,000 Gold** |
| **Mid-Tier Tab** | Tier 6 "good" stats (Primary Attributes, Damage) | **100,000 - 250,000 Gold** |
| **High-Tier Tab** | Tier 7 meta stats or Tier 6 + high Forging Potential | **500,000 - 1,000,000 Gold** |
| **God-Tier Tab** | Multiple T6/T7 meta stats (Crit Multi + Health) | **5,000,000+ Gold** |

### Key Stats to Watch For
When scanning your loot, look for these high-value modifiers that consistently fetch high prices:
*   **Critical Strike Multiplier:** Always in demand for hit-based builds.
*   **Hybrid Health:** Essential for survivability in high corruption.
*   **Level of [Skill]:** Specifically for popular meta skills like Explosive Trap or Runebolt.
*   **Experimental Affixes:** Found on items from Mages; these require specific Merchant Guild ranks to trade.

## Pricing Uniques: LP and Weaver's Will

Unique items follow a different valuation logic. A Unique with zero Legendary Potential (LP) is often worth the minimum listing price unless it is an exceptionally rare drop like *Omnis* or *Red Ring of Atalararia*. The real wealth lies in items with 2, 3, or 4 LP.

| Legendary Potential | Market Rarity | Pricing Logic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0 LP** | Common | Usually worthless unless the base rolls are perfect. |
| **1 LP** | Uncommon | Worth checking for high-demand Uniques (e.g., *Morningfrost*). |
| **2 LP** | Rare | Generally worth **250k to 2M gold** depending on the item. |
| **3 LP** | Very Rare | High-value items; always perform a manual **last epoch price check**. |
| **4 LP** | Ultra Rare | "Price is no object" territory; usually tens of millions. |

Weaver's Will (WW) items are also valuable. These items "level up" as you kill enemies, gaining random affixes. When selling a WW item, the total Weaver's Will number (ranging from 5 to 28) dictates the price. A high WW roll allows the item to potentially reach Tier 7 affixes on its own, making it highly desirable for players who don't want to engage in the Legendary crafting process.

## The Idol Market Strategy

Idols are frequently overlooked during a **last epoch price check**, yet they are some of the most consistent gold earners. Because Idols cannot be crafted, a "perfect" combination of two affixes is incredibly rare.

### High-Value Idol Combinations
*   **Small Lagonian Idols:** Health + Resistance (specifically Cold or Lightning).
*   **Stout Lagonian Idols:** Health + % Health.
*   **Large Idols:** Increased [Skill] Damage + Health.
*   **Adorned Idols:** Double Resistance or Mana + Armor.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If an Idol has an "Enchant" on it (from certain endgame mechanics), the auto-populate search feature may fail. You must manually uncheck the "Enchants" filter in the Bazaar UI to see comparable listings.

## Advanced Trading Tactics

To truly excel in the Merchant's Guild, you should utilize the **Rune of Creation**. If you find a Tier 7 item that is worth several million gold and has at least 1 Forging Potential remaining, you can use a Rune of Creation to duplicate it. This allows you to sell one copy and keep the other, or list both for double the profit. Note that this reduces the Forging Potential of both items to zero, so only do this on items that are already "complete" in terms of their affixes.

Additionally, remember that your ability to trade is capped by your **Merchant's Guild Rank**. 
*   **Rank 7:** Unlocks the ability to buy and sell Exalted items.
*   **Rank 8:** Unlocks the ability to buy and sell Uniques with Legendary Potential.

## FAQ

**Q: Why does my last epoch price check show "No Results Found" for a unique item?**

A: This usually happens because the auto-search is too specific. Try removing the "Roll" ranges for the implicit stats and focus only on the Legendary Potential (LP) count. Most buyers search for the name of the item and the LP, rather than specific stat rolls.

**Q: Is it better to sell items for a low price quickly or wait for a big sale?**

A: In the Merchant's Guild, your listing slots are limited. It is generally better to price items slightly below the market average to ensure a quick turnover. This frees up slots for new loot and keeps your gold flowing for upgrades.

**Q: Can I price check items while I am inside a Monolith?**

A: No, there is currently no way to perform a formal **last epoch price check** while in a combat zone. You must visit the Bazaar. However, you can use the 'Y' menu to check if your previous listings have sold while you are out adventuring.

**Q: What is the most important factor in an item's price?**

A: For Exalted items, it is the Tier level of the affix (T7 is significantly more valuable than T6). For Uniques, it is the Legendary Potential. For Idols, it is the synergy between the two random affixes. Always check these three factors first.
