## The Importance of Filtering in Shattered Omens (Patch 1.4)

The Shattered Omens update, also known as Patch 1.4, introduced several new item types and affix combinations that have shifted the meta. A standard filter from previous seasons will likely hide some of the most valuable new bases or fail to highlight the specific affixes required for 2026 builds. 

Loot filters in Last Epoch function on a "top-down" priority system. This means the game checks the first rule in your list; if an item matches, it follows that rule and ignores everything below it. Understanding this hierarchy is the difference between finding a Tier 7 Experimental affix and leaving it on the ground.

### Why Your Old Filter is Obsolete
1.  **New Base Types:** Season 4 introduced localized base items that offer unique implicit modifiers.
2.  **Affix Scaling:** Certain affixes now have higher weighting in the endgame, requiring recoloring for visibility.
3.  **Faction Specifics:** Depending on whether you choose the **Merchant's Guild** or the **Circle of Fortune**, your filter needs to prioritize trade value versus personal upgrade potential.

## How to Import and Setup Your Filter

Setting up a **last epoch season 4 loot filter** is a straightforward process, but doing it correctly is vital for efficiency. You have two main options: importing a community-made string or building your own from scratch.

### Step-by-Step Import Guide

3.  Select "Import Filter" and then "Paste Clipboard Content" if you have copied a string from a site like Last Epoch Tools.

## Building a Custom Rule Hierarchy

If you prefer to tailor your experience, building a custom **last epoch season 4 loot filter** allows for the most precision. You should structure your rules to ensure that rare crafting materials and high-value affixes are never missed while hiding the clutter of common items.

### 1. The "Hide All" Foundation
At the very bottom of your filter (the last rule), you should eventually set a rule to **Hide** all items of "Normal," "Magic," and "Rare" rarity. This ensures that as you progress into the endgame, your screen stays clear of items that are no longer useful for your level.

### 2. Highlighting Exalted and Unique Items
Above the "Hide All" rule, you must create rules to **Show** or **Recolor** high-tier items. 
*   **Uniques/Sets:** Always show these, perhaps recoloring them to a bright gold or purple to distinguish them from standard loot.
*   **Exalted Items:** Create a rule to show all Exalted items, but consider adding a sub-rule to highlight those with specific high-tier affixes (Tier 6 or Tier 7) that fit your build.

### 3. Affix-Specific Filtering
This is the most critical part of a **last epoch season 4 loot filter**. You want to highlight items that contain "Rare Affixes" such as:
*   +Level of [Skill Name]
*   Hybrid Health
*   Experimental Affixes (from Mages)
*   Class-specific modifiers (e.g., Rogue's "Glancing Blow" or Sentinel's "Block Effectiveness")

| Rule Priority | Action | Target | Logic |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1 (Highest)** | **Recolor (Red)** | Tier 7 Affixes | Never miss a **god-roll** item. |
| **2** | **Show** | Unique/Set Items | Essential for **Legendary Potential** crafting. |
| **3** | **Recolor (Green)** | Class Specific | Target items for your **current build**. |
| **4** | **Hide** | Low Level Bases | Clear screen of **outdated** gear. |

## Advanced Filtering for Factions

In Season 4, your faction choice significantly impacts how you should view loot. The **last epoch season 4 loot filter** you use should reflect whether you are looking for items to sell or items to wear.

### Circle of Fortune (CoF) Strategy
If you are in the Circle of Fortune, your goal is to find items with high **Legendary Potential (LP)**. Your filter should be set to highlight all Uniques with 2+ LP, as these are the core of your endgame power. You should also be more lenient with Exalted items, as you will need a large pool of them for the Soul Gambler and the Resonance system.

### Merchant's Guild Strategy
For trade-focused players, the filter should focus on "Market Value." Even if an item isn't good for your build, if it has a Tier 7 "Increased Health" affix on a good base, it is worth a significant amount of gold. Set your filter to highlight high-value generic affixes that are popular across all classes.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Being too strict with your filter in the Merchant's Guild can cost you millions in gold. Ensure you are showing high-value generic bases even if they don't match your class.

## Optimizing for Performance

A common issue in 2026 is "Filter Lag," where having too many complex rules (50+) can cause a slight stutter when large groups of enemies die simultaneously. To optimize your **last epoch season 4 loot filter**, follow these guidelines:
*   **Consolidate Rules:** Instead of having five separate rules for different weapon types, combine them into one rule with multiple "Sub-types" selected.
*   **Use Affix Groups:** Group similar affixes (e.g., all Resistances) into a single rule rather than filtering for each one individually.
*   **Level Scaling:** Use the "Level" condition to hide lower-tier items automatically as you level up, rather than manually updating the filter every five levels.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use my Season 3 filter for the Last Epoch season 4 loot filter?**

A: While it will technically function, it is not recommended. Season 4 (Patch 1.4) introduced new item bases and affixes that your old filter will likely ignore or hide. It is better to import a fresh Season 4 template and customize it.

**Q: How do I filter for items with Legendary Potential?**

A: Within the filter rules, add a condition for "Rarity" (Unique) and then add a second condition for "Legendary Potential." You can set the slider to show only items with 1, 2, 3, or 4 LP.

**Q: Why are some items still showing even though I have a "Hide" rule?**

A: Remember that Last Epoch filters work from the top down. If you have a "Show" rule for Exalted items at the top, and a "Hide" rule for specific weapon types at the bottom, the game will show the Exalted item because it met the first criteria. Move your "Hide" rules higher or add more specific conditions to your "Show" rules.

**Q: What is the best color to use for rare affixes?**

A: Most players use **Bright Red** or **Neon Pink** for Tier 7 affixes and **Bright Green** for class-specific skills. This creates a high-contrast visual cue that is hard to miss during intense combat.
